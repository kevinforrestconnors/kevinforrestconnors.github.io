academics

My interest in computers began at the age of five, playing Cro-Mag Rally on my dad's Macintosh Performa. At the age of 14, I started making algorithmic art in JavaScript. Since then, I've dabbled in lots of different languages in every paradigm, and I really enjoy learning languages.

I also like learning about languages. My fourth semester in college I took Introduction to Linguistics and decided to minor in it. In order to fulfill the elective requirements, I began taking advanced Spanish classes, culminating in 300 levels. The combination of formal, mathematical language study (Computer Science), natural language study (Spanish), and the study of languages caused me to reconsider what a language is. Richard Montague once said, “I reject the contention that an important theoretical difference exists between formal and natural languages.” In other words, any natural language can, in theory, be described completely mathematically!

My coursework caused me to be interested bridging the conceptual gap between formal and natural languages. This involves both improvements on programming languages and IDEs and also studying ways to improve natural language communication through technology.
